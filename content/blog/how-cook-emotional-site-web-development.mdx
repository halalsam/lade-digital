An emotional site isn't louder than the rest — it's more *intentional*. Every
transition, every pause, every reveal is timed to land at exactly the right
moment.

## The ingredients

1. **Restraint.** One typeface, a tight palette, lots of room.
2. **Timing.** Motion that matches the easing of the real world.
3. **Surprise.** A small, delightful moment the visitor didn't expect.

## The method

Build the quiet parts first. Get the typography and spacing right when nothing
is moving. Only then add motion — and add it sparingly, like seasoning.

The result is a site people remember without knowing quite why.
