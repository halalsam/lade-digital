A custom cursor is one of those tiny touches that makes a site feel
hand-crafted. Done well, it's invisible until it matters — then it scales,
blends, or labels itself to guide the eye.

## The idea

Track the pointer, lerp a follower element toward it each frame, and let
elements opt into behaviours via a `data-cursor` attribute.

## Why it works

- **Lerping** gives the follower a natural, weighted lag.
- **Blend modes** let it invert whatever it sits over.
- **Data attributes** keep the behaviour declarative and per-element.

Small in code, big in feel. That's the whole game.
