Great interfaces start long before a single line of code. They start with a
clear idea of how the page should *feel* — the rhythm of the type, the weight of
the negative space, the moment a button responds under the cursor.

## Start with the grid

Every editorial layout is a conversation between content and the empty space
around it. Block out your columns first, then let the type breathe inside them.

## Bring it to life

- Reveal headings as they scroll into view.
- Let primary actions invite a hover.
- Keep motion fast, soft, and purposeful — never decorative for its own sake.

Once the structure sings, the rest is detail work. And detail work is where the
craft lives.
